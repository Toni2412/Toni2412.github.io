Motivation

Hi, I’m Antonia Mühleck.

I’ve always had diverse interests. Growing up as the daughter of two art teachers, from earliy on I developed a passion for painting, photography, anything creative (some of those things I am presenting to you here). In school, I discovered my interest in maths and physics and later chose to study engineering.

Because I am also interested in social and political problems, and climate change is a big one, I chose the programme “Sustainable Systems Engineering” at the University of Freiburg. While I am very happy with that choice, I’ve always wished the programme would be more interdisciplinary.

Last year, I attended a research conference where I had an experience that really showed me that interdisciplinary collaboration is crucial to address the big problems. (I will tell you more about this story here) It became increasingly clear to me that I wanted to step out of my field, at least for the time being, and broaden my perspective. I believe that the “Design and Computation” Masters would be the perfect opportunity for me to do so!
