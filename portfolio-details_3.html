Airborne Wind Energy Conference 2024

In April 2024, I got the opportunity to join the Airborne Wind Energy Conference in Madrid. This was a very exciting experience and my first step into the “world of research.”

There was one specific moment that really stuck with me, and showed me how crucial interdisciplinary action and collaboration is for climate issues. In a research talk given by one of the organizers, the speaker described the social challenges involved in climate action. He then asked all engineers in the audience to raise their hands,and the entire audience proceeded to do so. Out of the remaining people, one was a psychologist, actively conducting research on the social acceptance of airborne wind energy. The others were investors.

While it’s expected that engineers lead in developing new energy technologies, the associated challenges clearly span multiple disciplines.

Today, climate change is largely a political and social issue. The technical solutions exist—now is the time to implement them. For that, collaboration across technical, social, and economic fields is essential. Engineers must understand the broader context to ensure that solutions are viable beyond the technical scope.
